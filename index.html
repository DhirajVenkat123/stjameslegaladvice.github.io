St James Legal Advice Centre

We are a charity providing free legal advice to those who cannot afford access to justice, empowering clients to understand their legal rights. We are made up of a dedicated team of volunteer solicitors, barristers, trainees and law students.

How We Can Help

We provide free legal advice on a range of issues, including:

  • Employment
  • Housing
  • Welfare benefits
  • Neighbour disputes
  • Consumer contracts

At all clinics, you can expect basic legal advice tailored to your issue. On Tuesdays and Wednesdays, we may also assist with drafting letters or emails, organising documents, and helping you complete forms.

We do not provide advice on criminal matters, debt, or consumer credit issues.

All clinics operate on a drop-in, first-come, first-served basis — appointments cannot be made, and we do not respond to emails requesting legal advice. To increase your chances of being seen, we recommend arriving early. Doors close 30 minutes before the advertised end time, or earlier if the clinic reaches full capacity.

Advice sessions are typically limited to 15 minutes and cover one legal issue per visit. If you need help with more than one matter, you may need to attend more than once.

Clinic Locations

We run three different legal advice clinics on separate days of the week, each based in a different North London location. The services available at each clinic differ depending on the expertise of the volunteers present and the facilities at each site.
